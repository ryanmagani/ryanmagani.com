My name is Ryan Magani. I am a student pursuing degrees in Computer Science and Business Administration at the University of Washington and will graduate in March of 2016.

I interned at Cobalt from June through August of 2014. Over the summer I developed the front- and backend of a web application used by Cobalt's customer service to manage which cars appear on a dealership's website. The backend performed CRUD operations to alter these inventories and exposed RESTful endpoints with which to be interfaced. The frontend was written in Angular and was designed based on feedback and needs of Customer Service teams.

I'm interested in computer science because I find that it presents an enjoyable challenge. Taking a complex problem and creating a set of instructions to solve it is often difficult, and this process and its result are greatly rewarding to me.

I am also studying business admin because I have come to realize that any work I do will involve others and having the ability to professionally communicate with my peers and work well in teams is invaluable.
